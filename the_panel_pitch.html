05 · The cast
06 / 16

Five agents. One hiring loop.

Each interviewer has their own personality, agenda, and evaluation criteria. They hand off context to each other. They argue about you at the end.

Round 1
Recruiter
Motivation, story coherence, basic fit. Warm but efficient. Listens more than talks.
Round 2
Hiring
Manager
Behavioral and leadership. "Tell me about a time you..." Probes for substance behind stories.
Round 3
Domain
Expert
Technical or domain depth. Calibrated to the company's actual standards.
Round 4
Bar
Raiser
Adversarial. Targets the weaknesses surfaced earlier. Raises the bar for "hire."
Always running · The product's edge
The Observer
Watches every answer. Maintains a candidate dossier. Routes targeted probes to the current interviewer. Catches contradictions across rounds. Feeds the final committee debate with concrete evidence — not vibes.

06 · The differentiator
07 / 16

The Observer is why this works.

Single-AI tools can't follow up because they have no memory of intent. The Observer is structural memory — it lives between agents and makes the panel feel like a real interview.

  • 01 Maintains a dossierClaims made, stories told, strengths shown, weaknesses exposed, topics dodged.
  • 02 Feeds targeted probes"Candidate claimed she led migration — Tech Lead should drill into specific decisions she made."
  • 03 Catches contradictions"Said she loves fast-paced to Recruiter, said she prefers planning to HM — flag for Bar Raiser."
  • 04 Hands off cleanlyGenerates structured notes between rounds: what we learned, what to dig into, what to leave alone.
  • 05 Powers the committeePresents evidence-based dossier so agents debate facts, not impressions.

08 · The workflow
09 / 16

How a session actually runs.

01
User selects company or industry
System routes to Tier 1, 2, or 3. Loads the right agent profile set. User uploads resume.
02
Round 1 — Recruiter
Conversational screen. Observer reads every answer, updates dossier in the background, feeds probes back to the Recruiter.
03
Handoff screen — the magic moment
User sees the Observer's note to the next interviewer. "Here's what we learned about you." Trust and transparency built in.
04
Rounds 2–4 — HM, Domain Expert, Bar Raiser
Each interviewer arrives armed with everything the Observer has built. Probes get sharper. Contradictions get caught. By Round 4, the Bar Raiser is hunting weaknesses surfaced earlier.
05
Committee deliberation
All four agents convene. Observer presents the dossier. They debate. They argue. User watches it happen live.
06
Final debrief — shareable
Decision (Strong Hire / Hire / No Hire / Strong No Hire). Evidence-based strengths and concerns. Action plan. Shareable LinkedIn debrief — built-in viral mechanic for company exposure.
The handoff and committee screens are the demo magic § Workflow

11 · The discipline
12 / 16

What we are not building.

Saying no to the obvious move is half the strategy. The market is full of products trying to do everything badly.

Real-time interview copilot
Contradicts our positioning ("get ready" vs "cheat live"). It's a latency engineering problem, not a multi-agent intelligence problem. Our edge doesn't apply. Plus: ethics, detection arms race, reputation risk.
Voice and video v1
Text-first ships in weeks, voice ships in months. Voice and video are v1.1. The multi-agent magic works in text and is more demonstrable.
Hiring-side product
Candidate-side has cleaner whitespace and better viral mechanics for exposure. Enterprise sales cycle would burn our timeline.
Profitability in v1
Goal is exposure and market validation, not revenue. Free meaningful tier. Monetization comes after we own the "panel interview" positioning.
Constraint is the design § Anti-roadmap
